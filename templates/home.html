Sudan Humanitarian Crisis

We have been working in Sudan since 2020, providing support to children and families affected by conflict, displacement, poverty, and hunger.

Sudan was facing a humanitarian crisis, where children and families were already experiencing high levels of poverty, malnutrition, disease outbreaks and climate-related shocks.

The conflict has compounded these existing crises, pushing the country into one of the worst humanitarian emergencies in the world, with parts of Sudan at risk of famine.

Over 20 million people face acute food insecurity, and over 30 million people - nearly two thirds of Sudan's population - currently need humanitarian assistance.

Children in Sudan are living through horrors no child should witness.

Since fighting broke out in April 2023, this has become the world's largest child displacement crisis.

Millions of children need our help right now.

The crisis children are facing

Displacement Crisis

Sudan’s children are facing a devastating humanitarian crisis driven by war, hunger and mass displacement.

More than 13 million people, including 7 million children, have been forced from their homes in what is now the world’s largest child displacement crisis, leaving families in overcrowded shelters without enough food, clean water or healthcare.

Food Insecurity

Hunger has reached catastrophic levels, with over half the population struggling to survive and 11.6 million children experiencing severe food insecurity.

Many young children are acutely malnourished and at risk of death without urgent treatment.

Conflict & Violence

In areas such as El Fasher and Kadugli, famine conditions are worsening, while thousands of children remain trapped under siege, unable to safely escape and exposed to violence, starvation and extreme suffering.

What this means for children

Children in Sudan are suffering the worst effects of the crisis, facing violence, abuse, displacement and the collapse of essential services.

Many have experienced attacks, sexual violence, abduction and forced recruitment by armed groups, while others have been separated from their families and left vulnerable to exploitation and trafficking.

Nearly 11 million children need education support as schools have been destroyed, closed or turned into shelters, and healthcare systems in conflict areas have largely broken down, cutting off access to lifesaving care.

Despite the urgent need, the crisis remains severely underfunded and humanitarian aid is struggling to reach affected children and families.

HOW WE'RE HELPING CHILDREN IN SUDAN

Healthcare

Operating mobile health clinics in communities hosting displaced families and supporting functioning health facilities to provide children with treatment for common and life-threatening illnesses, maternal and newborn care, and emergency responses to disease outbreaks such as cholera.

Nutrition support

Screening children for malnutrition, providing therapeutic care for severe acute malnutrition, and supporting mothers with optimal infant and young child feeding practices.

Water, sanitation and hygiene

Supporting families in displacement sites and conflict-affected communities to meet basic needs through food, access safe water and basic sanitation through water trucking, water storage facilities, and the distribution of hygiene kits.

Education

Supporting children to continue learning through schools and learning spaces, providing learning materials, setting up temporary education activities.

Child protection

Providing services to identify and support children at risk, offering psychosocial support, supporting family tracing and reunification, creating safe spaces.
