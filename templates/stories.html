Impact Stories of Transformation

When Florina arrived in a village near Khartoum, she met Amal, a twelve-year-old who had never attended school. Through Lend a Hand, a safe learning centre was created, offering education, meals, and clean water.

Within a year, Amal learned to read and write and now dreams of becoming a teacher. Her story reflects how access to education can transform not only a child’s future, but an entire community.

In a conflict-affected town in Syria, thirteen-year-old Yusuf had lost access to education and stability. Lend a Hand helped establish a support centre providing learning, counselling, and a safe routine.

With time, Yusuf regained confidence, rediscovered his passion for learning, and now hopes to become an engineer. His progress has brought renewed hope to his family and others around him.

In rural Kenya, 14-year-old Amina struggled to continue her education due to extreme poverty. Lend a Hand partnered with local volunteers to provide school supplies, meals, and mentorship support.

Today, Amina is one of the top students in her class and aspires to become a doctor, determined to help improve healthcare in her community.

In Iraq, 15-year-old Rahim was forced to leave school due to economic hardship. Through Lend a Hand’s learning initiative, he gained access to informal education classes.

Rahim has since caught up academically and now dreams of becoming a civil engineer to help rebuild infrastructure in his hometown.
